Stok Kangri

Trekking to Sandakphu mainly starts from 1,524 Bagdogra –Darjeelingmeters and therefore climbs up through the forests of giant magnolias, rhododendron, spruce and other trees of the sub-alpine region and more than 600 kinds of orchids, which are the largest to be found in any geographical climate. The best times to trek are April and May for the flora worshippers and autumn is the best season for people, who only trek to view mountains. In fact, Sandakphu is the home to a number of snow capped mountains like Makalu, Everest, Kumbhakarna, Lhotse, Chomolhari, Kanchenjanga in a single stretch.

Detail Itinerary:

Day-01:

Siliguri (NJP) - Manebhanjang: This is the first day of trek and the trek starts from Manebhanjang, a small village inhibited by the Sherpas and finishes at Tonglu. Tourists can spend the first night at Tumling, 11kms from Manebhanjang. During the trek, travelers can enjoy a wonderful view of Kanchendzonga and its neighboring peaks. One can even enjoy a clear view of the Nepalese valleys and the plains of North Bengal on a clear day.

Day-02:

Tonglu – Jaubari – Goiribans – Kaiyakatta – Kalipokhari: Next day, the trek continues from Tonglu to Gairibas. The total distance is 9km and it takes 2 hrs. The final destination is Kalipokhri and from this destination the road descends to Bikhaybhanjyang-valley of poison.

Day-03:

Kalipokhari – Sandakphu: From Kalipokhari, the track descends to Bikhebhangang. This is a steep climb of 4 kms and takes more than one hour. Here the mountain slopes are covered with silver fir trees and rhododendrons.

Day-04:

Sandakphu – Sirikhola: This day starts early and retraces the same route to reach Sabarkum starting from Phalut. The entire 7 km section is totally downhill. On this day, the travelers need to stay at Srikhola.

Day-05:

Sirikhola – Rimbik From Srikhola-Siliguri(NJP): The trek ends at Rimbik from where one can drive out to Siliguri through Rimbik.
