Deo Tibba Base Camp and Hamta Pass Trek

This is considered as one of the most delightful treks in the region of Manali. Located at 6001m, Deo Tibba is a renowned peak in the entire eastern Pir Panjal Range. Besides, Hamta Pass is known as the traditional route between Lahul and Kullu valleys. So, this trek takes the tourists to the wonderful foothills of Himalayas, known as the Pir Panjal Range to reach to remote Lahaul. The combination of green valleys below and barren landscape in the upper region makes this track awe-inspiring. Besides, the Hamta Pass Trek offers amazing scenic views of towering snow clad hills and mountains, lush green valleys.

Detailed itinerary:

Day-01:

Manali to Bogi Thach (2400m) - 3-4 hours: The trip starts from Manali and depart to the starting point at Jagatsukh. The destination is Bogi Thach that defines the culture of Himachal Pradesh.

Day-02:

Bogi Thach to Lamadugo (3000m) - 5-7 hours: Next day, the tourists head up left side of the camp and this day is a total hill walk that passes through several summer settlements of Gujars. After the river crossing, the climb is steep, but therefore, the track starts descending down to Lamadugo camp.

Day-03:

Lamadugo to Deo Tibba base (3475m) - 5-7 hours: The day starts with an uphill trek. The trek mostly goes up. From here the travelers reach to the camping place, known as Rouli Khouli, a huge meadow. From this place, the travelers can start heading down to the river and after crossing the river, it comes the next base camp, beside the Deo Tibba Mountain.

Day-04:

Rest day or excursion to Deo-Tibba glacier (3700m) - 3-4 hours: This is a complete relaxed day. Travelers head up to the glacier of Deo Tibba Mountain on this day if they want.

Day-05:

Deo Tibba base to Chhibba (3000m) - 5-6 hours: Schedule of this day starts with a gradual uphill trek starting from the left side of the camp while enjoying the great views of Himalaya. This also works as a great viewpoint. After spending half an hour there, travelers can go down for camping. Then the walk gradually starts downhill and after that there is a little steep down to the riverbed. After crossing one river crossing, there comes another one. So, the next camping place is at the confluence of two rivers.

To reach, first, the travelers need to cross the stream or the chhibba Nala and the track goes uphill. The valley is really narrow until reaching to a summer settlement at Jwara. The track climbs up for an hour after Jwara. The next base camp is at the shadow of Indrason peak (6000m).

Day-07:

Balugera to Sheraguru (3815m) via Hampta pass (4200m) - 5-6 hours: Next day, the camp starts from the right side of the valley and heads up to the pass. Both the sides of this track offer amazing view of the entire Himalayan range.

Day-08:

Sheraguru to Chatru and drive to Manali (Drive time: 4-5 hours, walk time: 3 hours): The morning starts with a river crossing. Therefore it leads to a drive that sends the travelers back to Manali over Rohtang Pass.
