Pin Parvati Pass Trek

The Pin Parvati pass trek is known as one of the most adventurous and difficult trek that needs loads of stamina and physical strength to complete this trek. This route mainly connects the dry dessert of Spiti valley located in Himachal Pradesh to the lush green kullu valley. This trek is mainly meant for people, who have solid experience in high altitude trekking. This trek will last for 7 days at the elevation of 15,000 ft.

Detailed itinerary:

Day-01:

The journey starts when the travelers arrive at Manikaran.

Day-02:

MANIKARAN TO KHIR GANGA (2960 mts): Next day, the journey commences from Manikaran and therefore the trail hikes to Khir ganga, a sulfur spring.

Day-03:

KHIR GANGA TO BHOJTUNDA (3285 mts): On the very next day, there is a stable rise out of the gorge towards Bhojtunda. In the route, the valley widens and the track crosses different delightful pastures in the summer days.

Day-04:

BHOJTUNDA TO THAKUERKUAN (3560 mts): This day, the trekking includes crossing two rivers through a pulley bridge over the Parwati river.

Day-05:

THAKURKUAN TO UDDI/ BAKKAR THACH (3850 mts): Several km above the valley from the Thakur kuan, the travelers will get the glimpse of snow- capped peaks in the neighborhood of the Pin Parwati Pass. Therefore, the track goes down to the Parwati River and then the travelers will reach to Bakkar thach, a steady rise through the grassy fields.

Day-06:

BAKKAR THACH TO MANTALAI LAKE (4116 mts): On this day, mostly the walk is one gradual ascent except the last section of 200m climb to reach to the Mantalai lake. This lake is the source of Parwati River and also works as a renowned pilgrimage.

Day-07:

LAY OVER DAY: This day is all about relaxing and acclimatizing the high altitude.

Day-08:

MANTALAI TO HIGH CAMP (4573 mtrs): This day, the journey is a 300m climb out to the high camp from the main valley floor. From the high camp, the viewers can enjoy a birdâ€™s eye view of the Parwati valley and full view of the Snow Peak, Pyramid Peak and also of the highest unnamed peak.

Day-09:

HIGH CAMP TO PIN PARWATI PAAS (5319 mts) AND TO PIN VALLEY CAMP (4050 mtrs): Now there is a sheer ascent of 200m over the boulders to the large snow field below the Pin Parwati Paas. Travelers can enjoy an alpine view to the snow-capped ranges towards the Kullu Valley. The next destination is the pin valley camp. So, after reaching to the Pin River base, the walk becomes really simple with some side streams crossings and gradual ups and downs.

Day-10:

PIN VALLEY CAMP TO MUD (3811 mts): In this journey, the view is completely different than the previous ones. Here the travelers need to cross some side streams.

Day-11:

MUD TO KAZA (3600 mts): Next day starts with a drive, which takes the travelers out of the pin valley through the villages. Therefore, this will take 3-4 hours to reach to Kaza in Spiti valley.

Day-12:

KAZA TO KIBBER (4270 mts): AND KI MONASTRY (4116 mts): In this day, the tourists will visit both the highest village of Kibber and the Ki monastery.

Day-13:

KAZA TO MANALI (2050 mts): The last step of the journey includes going over to passes Rohtang Pass and Kunzum La and therefore the travelers are dropped to Manali.
